Cal Newport is often quoted in places like The New York Times, often when productivity and office culture is being discussed. This book was written in 2019 and already feels dated, given the way that social media — actually, media in general — has further curdled and splintered. But the biggest oversight here is endemic to self-help books: it argues that the way to push back against products engineered and designed to be addictive is purely the individual’s responsibility. Not a word about properly regulating these products, not a word about collective action to force companies to change.
