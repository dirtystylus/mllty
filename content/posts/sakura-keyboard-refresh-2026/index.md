Got a bit bored and pulled my [Bakeneko 65](/posts/bakeneko65/) keyboard out of storage with the idea rotating it in with my [Mode 65](https://markllobrera.com/posts/mode65-2024-keyboard-build/) — except I found my RSI flaring up again after typing on it for a day. I guess I really am a sub-45g switch person now.

![](sakura-keyboard-refresh-2026-1.jpg "Original Gazzew Linear Thock switches, 57g springs")

So: a search for lighter switches. I ended up looking at Huano, a newer (to me) switch company that has a number of lighter-weight options. Perhaps in an unconscious echo of the cherry blossoms outside, I ended up choosing the [Sakura (V2) linear](https://milktooth.com/products/sakura) — 35g actuation/45g bottom-out, making it one of the lightest switches out there.

The Huanos were very tight in the board — they took a bit of force to get them into the PCB, and I ended up bending the prongs of two of the switches.

The new switches are really light, and a little bit scratchier than the [Keylin switches](/posts/mode65-2024-switch-swap/) in my Mode 65. Overall it’s a bit clackier/poppier than my Mode — I wish the whole board sounded a little bit deeper, but overall it’s an interesting change from the Mode. I plan to rotate keyboards every two weeks.
