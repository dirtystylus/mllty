First time seeing this on the big screen, part of [Bryn Mawr Film Institute’s](https://letterboxd.com/brynmawrfilm/) [100th anniversary](https://brynmawrfilm.org/series/bmfi-100/) celebration.

The Movie Jawn folks were in the lobby selling zines, and they [captured me](https://bsky.app/profile/moviejawn.com/post/3mjmh5mlx2c26) buying a copy of their [Summer 2023](https://www.moviejawn.com/shop/moviejawn-summer-2023) issue with _The Ghost & Mrs. Muir_ art on the cover.

I’ve seen this movie enough times at this point that the top-line pleasures are very familiar, and I tend to focus on the supporting characters like Dinah and Liz. Seeing it with a packed house is a very different experience, too — the laughs just hit harder.

Of the big three Grant/Hepburn comedies (_Holiday_, _Bringing Up Baby_, and _The Philadelphia Story_) I draw the most connection between _Holiday_ and this one, because both actively engage with the theme of class and how it affects marriage. I wish we had gotten more movies from the duo.
