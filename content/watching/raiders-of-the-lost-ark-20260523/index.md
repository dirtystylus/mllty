Caught a screening at the [Colonial Theater](https://thecolonialtheatre.com) in Phoenixville, PA. I ran into [Dan Santelli](https://letterboxd.com/kenpocourage/), head of film programming at the Colonial  (but perhaps more importantly, former clerk at my dearly departed local video store, [Viva Video](https://letterboxd.com/vivavideo/)).

This might be the first time I’ve ever seen it on a big screen? This movie is permanently connected to Betamax tapes in my brain. Still the model of excellence for action/adventure films, even if there are parts that have clearly not aged well (all the depictions of foreign cultures, and how Marion’s character is reduced to a literal bargaining chip in the back half of the movie, undercutting such a strong introduction to the character).

But: the action is so legible here, the practical effects and stunts put most modern productions to shame, and the whole thing just _moves_. I had a grand time.
