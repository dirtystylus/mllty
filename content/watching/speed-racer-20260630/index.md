I can’t remember if I saw this in the theater in 2008, but I remember seeing it that year and quickly putting it out of my head — it was too garish and frenetic for me at the time. I’ve revisited it once or twice since then, and each time I’ve come to appreciate it more and more — certainly for the colorful and kinetic visual language (that still feels like it is breaking new ground), but perhaps even more for the sincerity at its heart. Sticking it to the billionaire class was never more relevant than today.

The way _Speed Racer_ eschews realism and finds cinematic freedom has a spiritual cousin in the Spider-Verse movies (down to the way time flows in the story), something that stands apart from the dominant strain of superhero films from the past fifteen years. Lots of great stuff out there about the film’s visual storytelling out there: I had seen [Patrick Willem’s video essay](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vwh9ETdhrf4), but I also recommend B.C. Wallin’s essay at Bright Wall/Dark Room, [_Speed Racer_: Cubist Cinema](https://www.brightwalldarkroom.com/2023/10/17/speed-racer-cubist-cinema/).

Stray thought: seeing Benno Fürmann as Inspector Detector — is that how the Wachowskis ended up working with Tom Tykwer?

It took a while to get the 4K in my hands, because of preorder issues (feels like I wasn’t the only one). It looks and sounds great, can’t wait to dig into the extras. I want to rewatch this with my kids, both of whom have gotten into their own animation projects over the last couple of years.
